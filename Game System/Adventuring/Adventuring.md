# Actions
An action is something you do that does not have a trivial chance of success due to circumstances, limited knowledge, etc.

All actions require a [[Gameplay#Checks|check]] (sometimes more) to be resolved.

Actions always belong to (just) one of the following three categories:

- **Physical**: primarily related to body functions
- **Mental**: primarily related to one's mind
- **Spiritual**: primarily related to one's soul

## Skill Challenges (Encounters)

Any straightforward, non-trivial, encounter is resolved as a skill challenge.

Determine the reason and the objective(s) of the challenge; can be as simple as pass the bridge some brigands hold to convince the king to send troops into an abandoned fort to stop an imminent invasion.

Create a clock with a total equal to the [[|EL]]. Player actions advance the challenge clock by their effect. When the clock is complete, the challenge has been won.

Create a second clock with a total equal to the [PL](onenote:#Narrative&section-id={F4B5F533-AED8-4749-B5F7-808512A72D86}&page-id={6FBF75C7-AF6F-40AA-990B-749AD3293D18}&object-id={FFC62F14-5C4D-0B1F-3386-D3330F030950}&38&base-path=https://d.docs.live.net/73fbda111a4ef6fc/Documents/System%20Intelligence/Game%20System.one). Player failures advance this clock by their effect. If this clock is completed first, the challenge is considered a failure.

### Turns

A skill challenge may consist of one or multiple turns, depending on how things go - take turns until the encounter clock is finished or the encountered is failed.

During each turn, the following are resolved in order:

- [[|Lair and battlefield actions start]], giving their tells.
- The GM gives an outline of the current situation.
- All participants declare how they plan to act, from lower to higher [[Experience Tier (ET)|ET]].
- Players are allowed [intuition](onenote:Creatures.one#Aspects&section-id={EAB9ADFB-E6B8-4F73-ADBC-D294AE1A651D}&page-id={E86930DB-0FA8-4F4A-95A6-64D42CEC52FA}&object-id={D9374300-42B7-7908-37D6-F1D49EFE7EFB}&B&base-path=https://d.docs.live.net/73fbda111a4ef6fc/Documents/System%20Intelligence)/[perception](onenote:Creatures.one#Aspects&section-id={EAB9ADFB-E6B8-4F73-ADBC-D294AE1A651D}&page-id={E86930DB-0FA8-4F4A-95A6-64D42CEC52FA}&object-id={B495F7F1-0909-0B65-196F-8D66CC821A24}&19&base-path=https://d.docs.live.net/73fbda111a4ef6fc/Documents/System%20Intelligence)/etc checks to have higher [[Experience Tier (ET)|ET]] opponents declare their plans earlier - this is at a rate of one check for each participant to be affected and it usually has a hard difficulty as the targets are of higher [[Experience Tier (ET)|ET]].
	- ❗Note that these checks count against a player's number of checks allowed during a turn.
- [[Lair and battlefield actions]] are resolved and take effect as appropriate depending on all participants' actions.
- Players make checks according to their plans - each player is allowed a maximum number of checks equal to their ET. The [[Gameplay#Difficulty|difficulty]] of these checks is determined by the intention and the any target(s).
- The effect of successes is added to the player's clock while the effect of failed checks is added to the enemies' clock.
	- Some skills, like combat skills during a fight or social skills during a negotiation, may be used to inflict or mitigate [damage](onenote:Adventuring.one#Combat&section-id={BB36EB49-DF7D-4C75-918C-1839830A0B03}&page-id={4757EE33-5753-4938-B754-7A63CC5EF5B9}&object-id={3A22A001-C8F3-02D4-0900-77B4EB6FF788}&C&base-path=https://d.docs.live.net/73fbda111a4ef6fc/Documents/System%20Intelligence) instead of contributing towards the encounter clock.
	- Some enemies, usually the ones that matter (bosses, mythic, etc), can also make their own checks and these are applied to the failure clock in addition to the players' effect.
- Enemies deal automatic damage to the players equal to the [EL](onenote:#Narrative&section-id={F4B5F533-AED8-4749-B5F7-808512A72D86}&page-id={6FBF75C7-AF6F-40AA-990B-749AD3293D18}&object-id={FFC62F14-5C4D-0B1F-3386-D3330F030950}&2F&base-path=https://d.docs.live.net/73fbda111a4ef6fc/Documents/System%20Intelligence/Game%20System.one), split as needed based on the actions taken and their results.
- Existing non-instantaneous [[Health#Conditions|conditions]] are resolved.
- Describe what happened during the turn and apply [[Health#Conditions|conditions]] for received [damage](onenote:Adventuring.one#Combat&section-id={BB36EB49-DF7D-4C75-918C-1839830A0B03}&page-id={4757EE33-5753-4938-B754-7A63CC5EF5B9}&object-id={3A22A001-C8F3-02D4-0900-77B4EB6FF788}&C&base-path=https://d.docs.live.net/73fbda111a4ef6fc/Documents/System%20Intelligence) as appropriate.

## Tasks

Tasks are long, sometimes complex, actions that require more involvement than usual.

### Complexity

Each task features a complexity level, which determines how quickly the task can be finished.

Tasks related to magic or technology usually have a complexity equal to 10*ML/TL.

### Progress

To finish a task, you take actions that can contribute towards the task's completion. A task's progress is considered a resource score.

Each check triggers an accumulation check on success, or sometimes an expenditure check on a failure, towards the task's progress.

Sometimes, progress may not start at zero but at a specific level, especially when you are repairing or improving equipment and vehicles.

### Time Interval

You generally attempt checks at specific time intervals, depending on the task. During each time interval, you are generally allowed to take up to [ET](onenote:Creatures.one#Tiers&section-id={EAB9ADFB-E6B8-4F73-ADBC-D294AE1A651D}&page-id={8A107FB6-3D37-43FE-8691-EE17F24A9514}&object-id={878F4173-2DFA-49CC-A00B-96AF5D5D39B2}&21&base-path=https://d.docs.live.net/73fbda111a4ef6fc/Documents/System%20Intelligence) checks, but all must be of a different type, by different combinations of [adventuring realms](onenote:Adventuring.one#General&section-id={BB36EB49-DF7D-4C75-918C-1839830A0B03}&page-id={9E939D10-AB02-47BC-835B-304C783AD350}&object-id={3E868A79-F977-0857-1A9A-C33BA64003E7}&B&base-path=https://d.docs.live.net/73fbda111a4ef6fc/Documents/System%20Intelligence) and [aspects](onenote:Creatures.one#Aspects&section-id={EAB9ADFB-E6B8-4F73-ADBC-D294AE1A651D}&page-id={E86930DB-0FA8-4F4A-95A6-64D42CEC52FA}&end&base-path=https://d.docs.live.net/73fbda111a4ef6fc/Documents/System%20Intelligence).

### Completion

When the task's progress reaches its complexity, the task is finished.

# Adventuring Activities

## Foraging, Salvaging, Scrapping, Disenchanting…
All these are resolved in a similar way. You make an appropriate [[../Gameplay/Gameplay|check]] and spend the time required in each activity.
The [[../Gameplay/Gameplay#Difficulty & Degree of Success/Failure (DoS/DoF)|DoS]] of this check is then used as that target of an [[Resources#Accumulation|accumulation]] check for the appropriate [[Resources|resource(s)]].

#### Disenchanting
Disenchanting is destroying pre-existing ❗enchantments to get the materials back.

#### Foraging
Foraging is about harvesting natural materials, food, and/or clean water in the wilderness.

#### Hunting
Hunting is taking down prey, either by attacking directly or setting traps up.

#### Salvaging
Salvaging describes all activities required to strip useful materials from pre-existing constructions, like buildings or derelict vehicles.

#### Scrapping
Scrapping is disassembling equipment to their base material.
