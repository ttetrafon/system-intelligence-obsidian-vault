Your luck determines how easy you get it all.

Luck is a [[Resources|resource]] you gain periodically and you can spend during adventuring to make things easier for you.

# Spending Luck


You can spend luck any time, as described below. For each use, you spend an appropriate amount of luck immediately after your luck has taken effect.

- **Nudge Faith (2*#)**: Change your [[Gameplay#Checks|check's]] result by #.
- **Avoid Harm (5*#)**: Reduce incoming [[damage]] by # points.
- **Flashback (25)**: Invoke a [[Timekeeping#Flashback|flashback]] even when not normally allowed to. You must be able to attribute the flashback's happenings to luck, coincidence, providence, or happenstance, but not preparation.

 > For example, you may looking for a contact in the thieves guild to fence all your illegal treasures. You may declare that once, when growing up in the city streets, you were saved by the renowned Shadow Thief, and that he keeps tabs with your from time to time. He may be your link to the Thieves' Guild, or he may be able to point you to the proper direction.

- **I Know a Guy (33)**: Create an NPC on the fly that will help you somehow in your current situations. Devise your connection to them, as well as their personality, characteristics and history in broad strokes. The GM must approve of the details, and may even alter them.
- **Change Fate (50%; must be taken by whole PC group)**: Alter reality in some way beneficial to your current situation in some plausible way. You must describe the changes and what you expect to happen differently, but the GM has the final say in what and how things have changed for the story and the world in general.

> For example, you are in the middle of a quest to save an abductee from a cult, but you arrive too late to stop the sacrifice. You can use this ability to overrule that outcome, and instead say that something happened, like a monster attack or some disagreement between the cultists, and the sacrifice has been delayed, allowing you to arrive at the nick of time.

# Gaining Luck

You gain luck either at specified intervals or by suffering drawbacks. When doing so you can make an [[Resources#Expenditure/Accumulation|accumulation]] check, with its [[Resources#Target|target]] dependent on the situation.

- **Session Start (1)**: You gain some luck at the start of each session.
- **Adventure End (5)**: You gain some luck when you complete a [quest](onenote:https://d.docs.live.net/73fbda111a4ef6fc/Documents/System%20Intelligence/Game%20System.one#Narrative&section-id={F4B5F533-AED8-4749-B5F7-808512A72D86}&page-id={6FBF75C7-AF6F-40AA-990B-749AD3293D18}&object-id={A542EE9F-8672-0D32-11FC-9B2814F86061}&52).
- **Invoke Drawbacks (1-10)**: You can invoke some drawback in the current situation. You describe the drawback and how it will negatively affect the situation to the GM, who decides the details and the target for your luck accumulation.
