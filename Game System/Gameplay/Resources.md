Everything in this game is an abstract resource, [[../Characters/Aspects/Aspects.md|aspect]] scores, to your [[|wealth]], to the number of arrows you have at hand.

> For example, you have a wealth score instead of counting exactly how much money you have.

# Value & Score

A resource has a value of d%, and a score equal to 1/10 its level (rounded down).

> Your science 74 means your science score is 7.

# Expenditure/Accumulation

Spending or gaining resources happens by rolling a d% and comparing it to your resource level.

If you roll above your current level, you increase/decrease the resource.

Note that resources can end up with a level greater than 100. In such cases, a roll of 100 means you increase/decrease the resource regardless of its actual level.

## Change

When accumulating/spending a resource, the value of the resource is changed by the units of the expenditure/accumulation check.

When a score is above 100, it increases/decreases only by 1 point always.

> Through strength training, you may increase your movement (walk). You roll a d% which results in 93. You walk level increases from 74 to 75. If it was a fast paced game, your walk would have increased to 77 instead, due to the +3 from your roll of 93.

## Target

Sometimes an expenditure/accumulation check has a target.

When dealing with expenditure, generally the target must not be greater than the resource's current score.

In this case, reduce the level by target*5 and use that to calculate the result.

Furthermore, if you end up increasing/decreasing the resource, the target acts as the minimum change, regardless if the change is static or not.

> You are buying some expensive equipment for your stronghold, which imposes a target of 6 on your wealth expenditure (current wealth level 88). Because of the target, your wealth expenditure will be against 88-6*5=58. You roll the d% and get 62. Your wealth decreases by 6, which is the minimum, to 82.

# Split/Merge Resources

Splitting means you create a new stack with a score up to one less than the initial one.

Merging means you take a resource stack and add it to your own. Always consider the highest between the two as your initial resource.

In both cases, you make an expenditure/accumulation check for the initial resource, with a target equal to the secondary's score.
